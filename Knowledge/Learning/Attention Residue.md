# Attention Residue

> **Definition**
>
> Attention residue is the cognitive cost of switching from one task to another. Part of your attention remains focused on the previous task, reducing your ability to concentrate fully on the current one.

---

## Why it Matters

Attention residue is one of the biggest hidden obstacles to deep, focused work.

Even brief interruptions—checking email, replying to a message, or switching between unrelated tasks—can significantly reduce cognitive performance.

Understanding this concept explains why multitasking often feels productive but produces lower-quality work.

---

## Explanation

When you stop working on Task A before reaching a natural stopping point and immediately begin Task B, your brain continues thinking about Task A.

Although your body has moved to the new task, part of your mind has not.

This remaining mental focus is called **attention residue**.

The result is:

- Reduced concentration
- More mistakes
- Slower thinking
- Lower creativity
- Increased mental fatigue

The more frequently you switch contexts, the more attention residue accumulates.

---

## Practical Applications

### Work

- Batch emails instead of checking continuously.
- Group similar tasks together.
- Schedule uninterrupted deep work sessions.

### Learning

- Study one subject at a time.
- Avoid switching between books or courses every few minutes.

### Personal Life

- Finish conversations before checking your phone.
- Avoid constant social media interruptions.

---

## Examples

### Poor Workflow

```
Coding
↓

Email

↓

Teams Chat

↓

Coding

↓

Phone Call
```

Each interruption leaves attention residue behind.

---

### Better Workflow

```
90 minutes Coding

↓

15 minutes Email

↓

90 minutes Coding
```

Fewer context switches result in deeper concentration.
