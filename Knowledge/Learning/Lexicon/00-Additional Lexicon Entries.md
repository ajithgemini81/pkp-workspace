## Word / Phrase
### Quixotic

## Date Added
**21-JUL-2026**

## Pronunciation
* **Simple pronunciation:** kwik-SOT-ik
* **IPA:** /kwɪkˈsɒt.ɪk/
* **Syllable breakdown:** quix·ot·ic
* **Stress:** Second syllable (**SOT**)
* **Common mistake:** Do not pronounce it with a Spanish accent like *don key-HOH-tay* (e.g., "kee-HOE-tik"). In English, standard usage fully anglicizes it as **kwik-SOT-ik**.

## Part of Speech
Adjective

## Simple Meaning
Extremely idealistic, impractical, or chasing unrealistic dreams—often with noble intentions.

## Context
Commonly encountered in:
* Books & Literature
* Business (describing overambitious, unrealistic plans)
* Academic writing
* Political commentary & Journalism

## Memory Hook
Think of **Don Quixote** (the legendary fictional knight) charging full speed on his horse to fight giant monsters—except they were actually just **windmills**. It is a noble and brave effort, but completely impractical and delusional.

## Everyday Example
> "Trying to repaint the entire house by myself in a single weekend turned out to be a quixotic dream."

## Professional Example
> "Launching three brand-new product lines simultaneously without additional staffing was a quixotic venture that quickly drained company resources."

## Synonyms
* **Common:** Unrealistic, impractical, dreamy
* **Formal:** Idealistic, visionary
* **Advanced:** Utopian, starry-eyed, chimerical

## Antonyms
* Pragmatic, realistic, practical, sensible

## Word Family
* **Quixotically** (Adverb)
* **Quixotism** / **Quixotry** (Noun)

## Etymology
Originates directly from **Don Quixote**, the hero of the famous 1605 novel by Spanish author Miguel de Cervantes. Don Quixote reads so many romance stories about chivalrous knights that he loses his sanity and sets off to perform foolishly heroic deeds in a mundane world.

## Common Mistakes
Do not confuse **quixotic** with simply "foolish" or "stupid." A quixotic act usually has **noble, romantic, or lofty intentions** behind it; it fails because it is wildly out of touch with reality, not because it was done out of malice or pure ignorance.

## Quick Recall
When someone charges at windmills with noble goals, their quest is **quixotic**.

## Word / Phrase
### Quipped

## Date Added
**21-JUL-2026**

## Pronunciation
* **Simple pronunciation:** kwipt
* **IPA:** /kwɪpt/
* **Syllable breakdown:** quipped (1 syllable)
* **Stress:** Single syllable
* **Common mistake:** Do not pronounce the final "-ed" as an extra syllable (e.g., *kwip-id*). It ends with a crisp, silent "t" sound (/t/). It rhymes with *tripped*, *snipped*, and *slipped*.

## Part of Speech
Verb (Past tense of *quip*)

## Simple Meaning
Made a fast, witty, or humorous remark in response to something.

## Context
Commonly encountered in:
* Literature and Fiction (as a dialogue tag)
* Journalism and News profile pieces
* Podcasts and Interviews
* Everyday witty banter

## Memory Hook
Picture a **whip** snapping quickly. A person who **quipped** snapped back with a **quick, witty** comment before anyone else could react. *(Quip = Quick + Wit)*

## Everyday Example
> "I only run when someone is chasing me," she **quipped** when asked if she wanted to join the marathon.

## Professional Example
> "I guess our servers decided to take an early weekend," the IT manager **quipped** during the sudden software outage.

## Synonyms
* **Common:** Joked, remarked, teased
* **Formal:** Bantered, riposted
* **Advanced:** Retorted, jested

## Antonyms
* Stated plainly, declared, pondered, deliberated

## Word Family
* **Quip** (Noun / Present Tense Verb)
* **Quipping** (Present Participle)
* **Quippy** (Adjective - informal)

## Etymology
Originates in the 1500s, likely shortened from the Latin word *quidlibet*, meaning "anything you please" or "a subtle argument." Over time, it evolved to mean a sharp, clever, or playful side remark.

## Common Mistakes
Do not confuse **quipped** with simply *said* or *explained*. A person hasn't **quipped** unless their statement was brief, fast, and carried a touch of cleverness, dry humor, or sarcasm. You wouldn't say, *"I need to buy milk," he quipped.*

## Quick Recall
When someone snaps back with a quick, witty joke on the spot, they **quipped**.
